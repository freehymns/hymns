Asleep In Jesus!

Verse 1:
A-sleep in Je-sus! Bless-èd sleep,
From which none ev-er wakes to weep;
A calm and un-dis-turbed re-pose,
Un-bro-ken by the last of foes.

Verse 2:
A-sleep in Je-sus! Oh, how sweet,
To be for such a slum-ber meet,
With ho-ly con-fi-dence to sing
That death has lost his ven-omed sting!

Verse 3:
A-sleep in Je-sus! Peace-ful rest,
Whose wak-ing is su-preme-ly blessed;
No fear, no woe, shall dim that hour
That man-i-fests the Sav-ior’s pow'r.

Verse 4:
A-sleep in Je-sus! Oh, for me
May such a bless-èd ref-uge be!
Se-cure-ly shall my ash-es lie
And wait the sum-mons from on high.

Verse 5:
A-sleep in Je-sus! time nor space
De-bars this pre-cious hid-ing place;
On west-ern plains or north-ern snows
Be-liev-ers find the same re-pose.

Verse 6:
A-sleep in Je-sus! Far from Thee
Thy kin-dred and their graves may be;
But there is still a bless-èd sleep,
From which none ev-er wakes to weep.


---
Author: Margaret Mackay (1802–1887)
Tune: Rest


---Original
west-ern=In-dian
north-ern=Lap-land

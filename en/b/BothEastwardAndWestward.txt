Both Eastward and Westward

Verse 1:
Both east-ward and west-ward The mes-sage is sound-ing;
The Pro-mised of age-s, Mess-i-ah, is born!
The an-gels are sing-ing; The hills are re-sound-ing,
The choirs of the heav-ens Pro-claim Him this morn!
Be-hold, you will find Him In Beth-le-hem's man-ger
The Son of the Hig-hest, Most low-ly His birth!
He ent-ers this world in The guise of a stran-ger
For He does not val-ue The pomp of the earth.

Verse 2:
Oh vast con-de-scen-sion! The strong and e-tern-al,
The might-y Cre-at-or Whom ser-aphs a-dore;
Though one with His Fa-ther In glo-ry sup-er-nal,
Our flesh He has ta-ken And does not ab-hor!
Our God is now with us, His name is called Je-sus.
Sweet babe whose app-ear-ing The ang-els pro-claim!
From sin and from death He has come to re-lease us;
He bears all our sins by That beau-ti-ful name!

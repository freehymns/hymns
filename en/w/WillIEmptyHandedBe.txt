Will I Empty Handed Be?

Verse 1:
Will I emp-ty hand-ed be
When be-side the crys-tal sea
I shall stand be-fore the ev-er-last-ing throne?
Must I have a heart of shame
As I an-swer to my name,
With no works that my Re-deem-er there can own?

Verse 2:
What re-gret must then be mine,
When I meet my Lord di-vine,
If I've wast-ed all the tal-ents He doth lend,
If no soul to me can say,
"I am glad you passed my way,
For 'twas you who told me of the sin-ner's Friend."

Verse 3:
If my grat-i-tude I'd show
Un-to Him who loves me so,
Let me la-bor till the eve-ning shad-ows fall;
That some lit-tle gift of love
I may bear to realms a-bove,
And not emp-ty hand-ed be when comes the call.

Verse 4:
When the har-vest days are past,
Shall I hear Him say at last,
Wel-come, toil-er, I’ve pre-pared for thee a place?
Shall I bring Him gold-en sheaves,
Rip-ened fruit, not fad-ed leaves,
When I see the bless-èd Sav-ior face to face?

Verse 5:
When the books are o-pened wide,
And the deeds of all are tried,
May I have a re-cord whit-er than the snow?
When my race on earth is run,
May I hear Him say, Well done,
Take the crown that Love im-mor-tal doth be-stow?


---
Author: Neal A. McAulay, 1904

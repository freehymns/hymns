With Joy We Meditate The Grace

Verse 1:
With joy we med-i-tate the grace
Of our high priest a-bove:
His heart is made of ten-der-ness,
His ver-y name is love.

Verse 2:
Touched with a sym-pa-thy with-in,
He knows our fee-ble frame;
He knows what sore temp-ta-tions mean,
For He has felt the same.

Verse 3:
But spot-less, in-no-cent, and pure,
The great Re-deem-er stood,
While Sa-tan’s fi-'ry darts He bore,
And did re-sist to blood.

Verse 4:
He in His days of fee-ble flesh
Poured out His cries and tears,
And in His meas-ure feels a-fresh
What ev-'ry mem-ber bears.

Verse 5:
He’ll nev-er quench the smok-ing flax,
But raise it to a flame;
The bruis-èd reed He nev-er breaks,
Nor scorns the mean-est name.

Verse 6:
Then let our hum-ble faith ad-dress
His mer-cy and His pow'r;
We shall ob-tain de-liv-'ring grace
In the dis-tress-ing hour.


---
Author: Isaac Watts (1674–1748)
Tune: Emmanuel

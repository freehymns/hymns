What Grace, O Lord, And Beauty Shone

Verse 1:
What grace, O Lord, and beau-ty shone
A-round Thy steps be-low!
What pa-tient love was seen in all
Thy life and death of woe!

Verse 2:
For-ev-er on Thy bur-dened heart
A weight of sor-row hung;
Yet no un-gen-tle mur-m'ring word
Es-caped Thy si-lent tongue.

Verse 3:
Thy foes might hate, de-spise, re-vile,
Thy friends un-faith-ful prove;
Un-wea-ried in for-give-ness still,
Thy heart could on-ly love.

Verse 4:
Oh, give us hearts to love like Thee,
Like Thee, O Lord, to grieve
Far more for oth-er's sins than all
The wrongs that we re-ceive.

Verse 5:
One with Thy-self, may ev-'ry eye
In us, Thy breth-ren, see
That gen-tle-ness and grace that spring
From un-ion, Lord, with Thee.


---
Author: Edward Denny (1796-1889)
Tune: Sawley

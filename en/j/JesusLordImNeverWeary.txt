Jesus, Lord, I'm Never Weary

Verse 1:
Je-sus, Lord, I'm nev-er wea-ry
Look-ing on Thy cross and shame;
Gaz-ing there I seem so near Thee,
Dear to me each throb of pain.
Ev-er near Thee, ev-er near Thee,
Ling'r-ing here I would re-main.

Verse 2:
Lit-tle cared I for the an-guish
Of Thy bit-ter, bit-ter cry;
Left a-lone, Lord, there to lan-guish,
None to share Thy part-ing sigh.
All for-sak-en! All for-sak-en!
Left a-lone, O Lord, to die.

Verse 3:
Je-sus, Sav-ior, I have found Thee
All my ut-most need re-quired;
In Thy-self, Lord, Thou hast found me
All Thy lov-ing heart de-sired.
I would praise Thee I would praise Thee 
From my soul by love in-spired.

Verse 4:
All my sins were laid up-on Thee,
All my guilt was on Thee laid,
And the blood of Thine a-tone-ment
All my ut-most debt has paid.
Gra-cious Sav-ior, gra-cious Sav-ior,
I be-lieve, for Thou hast said.

Verse 5:
Thine al-might-y arms a-round me,
And my head is on Thy breast,
For my wea-ry soul has found Thee
Such a per-fect, per-fect rest.
Gra-cious Sav-ior, gra-cious Sav-ior,
Now I know that I am blest.


---
Author: Mrs. Thompson
Tune: Balm

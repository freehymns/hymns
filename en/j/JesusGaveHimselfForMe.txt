Jesus Gave Himself for Me

Verse 1:
Lord, Thy love has sought and found us
Wand-'ring in this de-sert wide;
Thou hast thrown Thine arms a-round us,
For us suf-fered, bled, and died.
Sing, my soul! He lov-èd thee,
Je-sus gave Him-self for me.

Verse 2:
Hark! what sounds of bit-ter weep-ing
From yon lone-some gar-den sweep;
'Tis the Lord His vig-il keep-ing,
While His foll-'wers sink in sleep.
Ah, my soul, He lov-èd thee,
Yes, He gave Him-self for me.

Verse 3:
He is speak-ing to His Fa-ther,
Tast-ing deep that bit-ter cup,
Yet He takes it, will-ing rath-er
For our sakes to drink it up.
Oh, what love! He lov-èd me!
Gave Him-self, my soul, for thee.

Verse 4:
Then that clos-ing scene of an-guish:
All God's waves and bil-lows roll
O-ver Him, there left to lan-guish
On the cross, to save my soul.
Match-less love! how vast, how free,
Je-sus gave Him-self for me.

Verse 5:
Hark a-gain! His cries are wak-ing
Echo-es on dark Cal-v'ry's hill;
God, my God, art Thou for-sak-ing
Him who al-ways did Thy will?
Ah, my soul! it was for thee,
Yes! He gave Him-self for me.

Verse 6:
Lord, we joy, Thy toils are end-ed,
Glad Thy suf-f'ring time is o'er;
To Thy Fa-ther's throne as-cend-ed,
There Thou liv'st, to die no more.
Yes, my soul, He lives for thee,
He who gave Him-self for me.

Verse 7:
Lord, we wor-ship and a-dore Thee
For Thy rich, Thy match-less grace;
Per-fect soon in joy be-fore Thee,
We shall see Thee face to face.
Yet e'en now our song shall be,
Je-sus gave Him-self for me.

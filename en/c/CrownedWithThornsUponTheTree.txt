Crowned With Thorns Upon The Tree

Verse 1:
Crowned with thorns up-on the tree,
Si-lent in Thine ag-o-ny;
Dy-ing, crushed be-neath the load
Of the wrath and curse of God.

Verse 2:
On Thy pale and suf-f'ring brow,
Mys-ter-y of love and woe;
On Thy grief in great a-maze,
Sav-ior, I would fix my gaze!

Verse 3:
On Thy ho-ly, lov-ing breast
Thou dost bid the wea-ry rest;
Rest there from the world’s false ways,
Rest there from its van-i-ties.

Verse 4:
Rest in par-don and re-lief,
From the load of guilt and grief,
Rest in Thy re-deem-ing blood,
Rest in per-fect peace with God.

Verse 5:
Sin a-ton-ing Sac-ri-fice,
Thou art pre-cious in my eyes;
Thou a-lone my rest shall be,
Now and through e-ter-ni-ty.


---
Author: H. Grattan Guiness (1835–1910)
Tune: Dunstan

---Original
in great a-maze=and sore a-maze
my eyes=mine eyes

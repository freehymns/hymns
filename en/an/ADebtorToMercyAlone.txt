A Debtor to Mercy Alone

Verse 1:
A debt-or to mer-cy a-lone,
Of cov-e-nant mer-cy I sing,
Nor fear, with God's right-eous-ness on,
My per-son and off-'rings to bring.
The ter-rors of law and of God
With me can have noth-ing to do;
My Sav-ior's o-bed-ience and blood
Hide all my trans-gres-sions from view.

Verse 2:
The work which His good-ness be-gan,
The arm of His strength will com-plete;
His prom-ise is Yea and A-men,
And nev-er was for-feit-ed yet.
Things fu-ture, nor things that are now,
Not all things be-low or a-bove,
Can make Him His pur-pose fore-go,
Or sev-er my soul from His love.

Verse 3:
My name from the palms of His hands
E-ter-ni-ty will not e-rase;
Im-pressed on His heart, it re-mains
In marks of in-del-i-ble grace.
Yes! I to the end shall en-dure,
As sure as the ear-nest is giv'n;
More hap-py, but not more se-cure,
The souls of the bless-ed in heav'n.

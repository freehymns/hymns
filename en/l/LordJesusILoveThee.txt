Lord Jesus, I Love Thee

Verse 1:
Lord Je-sus, I love Thee, I know Thou art mine,
My Rock and my For-tress, my Sure-ty di-vine,
My gra-cious Re-deem-er, my song shall be now:
'Tis Thou who art wor-thy, Lord Je-sus, 'tis Thou.

Verse 2:
I love Thee be-cause Thou hast first lov-èd me
And pur-chased my par-don on Cal-va-ry's tree;
I love Thee for wear-ing the thorns on Thy brow:
'Tis Thou who art wor-thy, Lord Je-sus, 'tis Thou.

Verse 3:
I'd love Thee in life, I would love Thee in death,
And praise Thee as long as Thou lend-est me breath,
And sing, should the death-dew lie cold on my brow:
'Tis Thou who art wor-thy, Lord Je-sus, 'tis Thou.

Verse 4:
And when the bright morn of Thy glo-ry shall come,
And chil-dren as-cend to the Fa-ther's glad home,
I'll shout, with Thy like-ness im-pressed on my brow,
'Tis Thou who art wor-thy, Lord Je-sus, 'tis Thou.


---
Author: William R. Featherstone
Tune: Gordon

Lord, E'en To Death Thy Love Could Go

Verse 1:
Lord, e'en to death Thy love could go,
A death of shame and loss,
To van-quish for us ev-'ry foe,
And break the strong man's force.

Verse 2:
Oh, what a load was Thine to bear,
A-lone in that dark hour,
Our sins in all their ter-ror there,
God's wrath and Sa-tan's pow'r!

Verse 3:
The storm that bowed Thy bless-èd head
Is hushed for-ev-er now,
And rest di-vine is ours in-stead,
Whilst glo-ry crowns Thy brow.

Verse 4:
With-in the Fa-ther's house on high
We soon shall sing Thy praise,
But here, where Thou didst bleed and die,
We learn that song to raise.


---
Author: H. Rossier (1834-1928)
Translator: C. A. Wellesley
Tune: Dublin

Sun Of My Soul

Verse 1:
Sun of my soul, Thou Sav-ior dear,
It is not night if Thou be near;
O may no earth-born cloud a-rise
To hide Thee from Thy serv-ant’s eyes.

Verse 2:
When the soft dews of kind-ly sleep
My wea-ried eye-lids gen-tly steep,
Be my last thought, how sweet to rest
For-ev-er on my Sav-ior’s breast.

Verse 3:
A-bide with me from morn till eve,
For with-out Thee I can-not live;
A-bide with me when night is nigh,
For with-out Thee I dare not die.

Verse 4:
If some poor wan-d'ring child of Thine
Has spurned to-day the voice di-vine,
Now, Lord, the gra-cious work be-gin;
Let him no more lie down in sin.

Verse 5:
Watch by the sick, en-rich the poor
With bless-ings from Thy bound-less store;
Be ev-'ry mourn-er’s sleep to-night,
Like in-fants’ slum-bers, pure and right.

Verse 6:
Come near and bless us when we wake,
Ere through the world our way we take,
Till in the o-cean of Thy love
We lose our-selves in Heav'n a-bove.


---
Author: John Keble, 1820
Tune: Hursley

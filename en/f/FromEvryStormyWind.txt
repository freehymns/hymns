From Ev'ry Stormy Wind

Verse 1:
From ev-'ry storm-y wind that blows,
From ev-'ry swell-ing tide of woes,
There is a calm, a sweet re-treat;
'Tis found be-fore the mer-cy seat.

Verse 2:
There is a place where mer-cy sheds
The oil of glad-ness on our heads,
A place than all be-side more sweet:
It is the heav'n-ly mer-cy seat.

Verse 3:
There is a spot where souls u-nite,
And saint meets saint in heav'n-ly light;
Though sun-dered far, by faith they meet
Be-fore the com-mon mer-cy seat.

Verse 4:
Ah, whith-er could we flee for aid
When tempt-ed, des-o-late, dis-mayed?
Or how the hosts of hell de-feat,
Had suf-f'ring saints no mer-cy seat?

Verse 5:
Thith-er by faith we'd up-ward soar,
Let time and sense seem all no more;
For free-ly God our souls can greet
Where glo-ry crowns the mer-cy seat.


---
Author: Hugh Stowell (1799-1865)
Tune: Retreat

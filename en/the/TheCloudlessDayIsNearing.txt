The Cloudless Day Is Nearing

Verse 1:
The cloud-less day is near-ing,
When Thou, O Lord, wilt come,
Thy rad-iant beau-ty wear-ing,
To take Thy peo-ple home!
Bright hosts on hosts a-round Thee
Shall catch Thy liv-ing rays,
And all who once have found Thee
Breathe out new songs of praise.

Verse 2:
But how shall I then know Thee
A-mid those hosts a-bove?
What to-kens true will show me
The ob-ject of my love?
Thy glo-ries, all ex-cel-ling,
In pure ef-ful-gence shine;
But glo-ry in Thee dwell-ing
Will ne’er pro-claim Thee mine.

Verse 3:
Thy wounds, Thy wounds, Lord Je-sus,
Those deep, deep wounds will tell
The sac-ri-fice that frees us
From self, and death, and hell!
These link Thee once for ev-er
With all who own Thy grace;
No hand these bonds can sev-er,
No hand these scars ef-face.

Verse 4:
O Je-sus! Lord most bless-èd,
Thou Lamb of God di-vine,
Thou stand-est forth con-fess-èd,
I, won-d’ring, claim Thee mine.
I wor-ship and a-dore Thee,
Trans-port-ed with Thy love;
Pros-trate my-self be-fore Thee,
And dwell in light a-bove.

Author: C. A. H.

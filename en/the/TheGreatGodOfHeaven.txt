The Great God of Heaven

Verse 1:
The great God of Hea-ven is come down to earth,
The son of a vir-gin, who gave Him His birth; 
Yet Son of the Al-might-y Fa-ther a-lone;
He sleeps in a man-ger; He reigns on the throne.

Verse 2:
A babe on the breast of a maid-en He lies,
Yet sits with the Fa-ther on high in the skies;
Be-fore Him their fac-es the ser-a-phim hide,
While Jo-seph looks long and is not terr-if-ied.

Verse 3:
Lo! here is Em-man-u-el, here is the Child,
The Son that was prom-ised to Ma-ry so mild;
Whose pow'r and dom-in-ion shall ev-er in-crease,
The Prince that shall rule o’er a king-dom of peace.

Verse 4:
The Won-der-ful Coun-sel-or, bound-less in might,
The Fa-ther’s own im-age, full beam of His light;
Be-hold Him now wear-ing the like-ness of man,
A weak, help-less ba-by who meas-ures a span.

Verse 5:
O Won-der of won-ders, which none can un-fold:
The An-cient of Days is an hour or two old;
The Ma-ker of all things is made of earth's dust,
The God an-gels laud, be-comes man all can trust.

Verse 6:
The Word in the bliss of the God-head re-mains,
Yet takes on our weak-ness to suf-fer our pains;
He is what He was, and for-ev-er shall be,
But is what He was not, for you and for me.

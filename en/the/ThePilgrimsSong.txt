The Pilgrim's Song

Verse 1:
My rest is in Heav-en; my rest is not here;
Then why should I mur-mur when tri-als are near?
Be hushed, my dark spir-it! the worst that can come
But short-ens my jour-ney, and has-tens me home.

Verse 2:
It is not for me to be seek-ing my bliss
And build-ing my hopes in a re-gion like this:
I look for a cit-y which hands have not piled;
I pant for a coun-try by sin un-de-filed.

Verse 3:
The thorn and the this-tle a-round me may grow;
I would not lie down up-on ros-es be-low:
I ask not my por-tion, I seek not a rest,
Till I find sweet qui-et on Je-sus’-s breast.

Verse 4:
Af-flic-tions may damp me, they can-not de-stroy:
One glimpse of His love turns them all in-to joy;
The bit-ter-est tears, if He smile but on them,
Like dew in the sun-shine, grow dia-mond and gem.

Verse 5:
Let doubt then, and dan-ger, my prog-ress op-pose;
They on-ly make Heav-en more sweet at the close.
Come joy, or come sor-row, what-e’er may be-fall,
An hour with my God will make up for it all.

Verse 6:
A scrip on my back, and a staff in my hand,
I march on in haste through an en-e-my’s land:
The road may be rough, but it can-not be long;
I’ll smooth it with hope, and I’ll cheer it with song.

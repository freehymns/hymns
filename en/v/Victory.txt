Victory

Verse 1:
O lead me to the Man that died,
Who all God's na-ture glo-ri-fied;
De-scend-ing to the depths of woe;
And for us van-quished ev-'ry foe!

Verse 2:
O lead me to the emp-ty tomb
His death has robbed of all its gloom;
He's ris'n! the Lord of life and peace,
And holds me in His fond em-brace.

Verse 3:
O lead me up to heav-en's height,
To see the Lord en-throned in light;
That gaz-ing on His glo-ry there,
I may re-flect His im-age here.

Verse 4:
O lead me to that meet-ing rare,
so of-ten longed for in the air;
Then, then, His bless-ed face I'll see,
And praise Him through e-ter-ni-ty.

Verse 5:
O lead me on to Zi-on's hill,
To see the Lord His Word ful-fil;
His glor-ious King is sit-ting there,
Rul-ing o'er earth and sea and air.

Verse 6:
O lead me to that scene sub-lime,
Pre-pared by God be-fore all time;
Sin, death, and night have passed a-way;
Light, life, and love are there to stay.

Verse 7:
There the E-ter-nal Three in One,
Blest Fa-ther, Spir-it and the Son,
Rest un-dis-turbed for-ev-er-more;
I won-der, wor-ship, and a-dore!

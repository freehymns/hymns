I’m But A Stranger Here

Verse 1:
I’m but a stran-ger here,
Heav’n is my home;
Earth is a de-sert drear,
Heav’n is my home.
Dan-ger and sor-row stand
Round me on ev-'ry hand;
Heav’n is my fa-ther-land,
Heav’n is my home.

Verse 2:
What though the tem-pest rage,
Heav’n is my home;
Short is my pil-grim-age,
Heav’n is my home;
Time’s wild and win-try blast
Soon shall be o-ver past;
I shall reach home at last,
Heav’n is my home.

Verse 3:
There at my Sav-ior’s side
Heav’n is my home;
I shall be glo-ri-fied,
Heav’n is my home.
There are the good and blest,
Those I loved most and best;
There, I shall ev-er rest,
Heav’n is my home.

Verse 4:
There-fore I mur-mur not,
Heav’n is my home;
What-e’er my earth-ly lot,
Heav’n is my home;
And I shall sure-ly stand
There at my Lord’s right hand.
Heav’n is my fa-ther-land,
Heav’n is my home.


---
Author: Thomas R. Taylor, 1836
Tune: StEdmund
Alt Tune: Bethany

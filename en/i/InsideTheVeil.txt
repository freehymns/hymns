Inside the Veil

Verse 1:
Through Thy pre-cious bod-y bro-ken
In-side the veil;
O what words to sin-ners spo-ken
In-side the veil!
Pre-cious as the blood that bought us,
Per-fect as the love that sought us,
Ho-ly as the Lamb that brought us
In-side the veil!

Verse 2:
When we see Thy love un-shak-en
Out-side the camp;
Scorned by man, by God for-sak-en,
Out-side the camp;
Thy loved cross a-lone can charm us,
Shame need now no more a-larm us,
Glad we fol-low, naught can harm us
Out-side the camp.

Verse 3:
Lamb of God, through Thee we en-ter
In-side the veil;
Cleansed by Thee, we bold-ly ven-ture
In-side the veil;
Not a stain; a new cre-a-tion;
Ours is such a full sal-va-tion;
Low we bow in ad-o-ra-tion
In-side the veil.

Verse 4:
Un-to Thee, the home-less stran-ger
Out-side the camp,
Forth we has-ten, fear no dan-ger
Out-side the camp.
Thy re-proach, far rich-er treas-ure
Than all E-gypt’s boast-ed pleas-ure;
Drawn by love that knows no meas-ure,
Out-side the camp.

Verse 5:
Soon Thy saints shall all be gath-ered
In-side the veil;
All at home, no more be scat-tered,
In-side the veil.
Naught from Thee our hearts shall sev-er;
We shall see Thee, grieve Thee nev-er;
"Praise the Lamb!" shall sound for ev-er
In-side the veil!


---
Author: Elizabeth Dark, 1872
Tune: Evensong

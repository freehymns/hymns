I Think, When I Read That Sweet Story

Verse 1:
I_ think, when I read that sweet sto-ry of old,
When_ Je-sus was here a-mong men,
How He called lit-tle chil-dren as lambs to His fold,
I should like to have been with them then.

Verse 2:
I_ wish that His hands had been placed on my head,
That His arms had been thrown a-round me,
And that I might have seen His kind look when He said,
Let the lit-tle ones come un-to Me.

Verse 3:
In that beau-ti-ful place He has gone to pre-pare
For_ all who are washed and for-giv’n;
So_ man-y dear chil-dren shall be with Him there,
For of such is the king-dom of Heav’n.

Verse 4:
I_ long for the joy of that glo-ri-ous time,
The_ sweet-est and bright-est and best,
When the dear lit-tle chil-dren of ev-er-ry clime
Shall= crowd to His arms and be blest.


---
Author: Jemima T. Luke, 1841
Tune: SweetStory

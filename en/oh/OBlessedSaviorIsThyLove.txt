O Blessed Savior, Is Thy Love

Verse 1:
O bless-èd Sav-ior, is Thy love
So great, so full, so free?
Fain would we have our thoughts, our hearts,
Our lives en-gaged with Thee.

Verse 2:
We love Thee for the glor-ious worth
Which in Thy-self we see;
We love Thee for that shame-ful cross,
En-dured so pa-tient-ly.

Verse 3:
No man of great-er love can boast
Than for his friend to die;
Thou for Thine en-e-mies wast slain!
What love with Thine can vie?

Verse 4:
Though in the ver-y form of God,
With heav'n-ly glo-ry crowned,
Thou didst a Ser-vant's form as-sume,
Be-set with sor-row round.

Verse 5:
Thou wouldst like sin-ful man be made
In ev-'ry-thing but sin,
That we, as like Thee might be-come,
As we un-like had been:

Verse 6:
O Lord, we treas-ure in our souls
The mem-'ry of Thy love;
And ev-er shall Thy name to us
A grate-ful o-dor prove.


---
Author: Joseph Stennett (1663-1713)
Tune: Dublin

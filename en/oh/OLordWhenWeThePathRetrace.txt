O Lord, When We The Path Retrace

Verse 1:
O Lord, when we the path re-trace
Which Thou on earth hast trod,
To man Thy won-drous love and grace,
Thy faith-ful-ness to God;

Verse 2:
Thy love, by man so sore-ly tried,
Proved strong-er than the grave;
The ver-y spear that pierced Thy side
Drew forth the blood to save;

Verse 3:
Faith-ful a-midst un-faith-ful-ness,
'Mid dark-ness on-ly light,
Thou didst Thy Fa-ther's name con-fess,
And in His will de-light;

Verse 4:
Un-moved by Sa-tan's sub-tle wiles,
Or suf-f'ring shame and loss,
Thy path, un-cheered by earth-ly smiles,
Led on-ly to the cross:

Verse 5:
We won-der at Thy low-ly mind,
And fain would like Thee be,
And all our rest and pleas-ure find
In learn-ing, Lord, of Thee.


---
Author: James G. Deck (1807-1884)
Tune: Lynnwood

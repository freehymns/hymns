O Lord And Savior, We Recline

Verse 1:
O Lord and Sav-ior, we re-cline
On that e-ter-nal love of Thine;
Thou art our rest, and Thou a-lone
Re-main-est when all else is gone.

Verse 2:
Yes! "Thou re-main-est": sea and land,
E'en heav'n shall pass, but Thou shalt stand;
Un-dimmed, Thy rad-ian-cy ap-pears,
Change-less through all the chang-ing years.

Verse 3:
Lord, on the throne Thy love's the same
As once up-on Thy cross of shame;
In low-li-ness Thou cam'st to die,
Thou liv-est now for us on high.

Verse 4:
We see Thee there, the Lamb once slain;
Thy loss was our e-ter-nal gain;
We see Thee crowned with glo-ry now,
No shade on Thy vic-tor-ious brow.

Verse 5:
The shad-ow dwelt on Cal-v'ry's tree,
And now Thine un-veiled face we see;
Praise be to God for that blest word,
That "Thou re-main-est," bless-ed Lord!


---
Author: Mrs. E. Helyer, 1891
Tune: Hesperus

Tell Me The Stories Of Jesus

Verse 1:
Tell me the sto-ries of Je-sus
I love to hear;
Things I would ask Him to tell me
If He were here;
Scenes by the way-side,
Tales of the sea,
Sto-ries of Je-sus,
tell them to me.

Verse 2:
First let me hear how the chil-dren
Stood round His knee,
And I shall fan-cy His bless-ing
Rest-ing on me;
Words full of kind-ness,
Deeds full of grace,
All in the love light
of Je-sus’ face.

Verse 3:
Tell me, in ac-cents of won-der,
How rolled the sea,
Toss-ing the boat in a tem-pest
On Gal-i-lee;
And how the Mak-er,
Read-y and kind,
Chid-ed the bil-lows,
And hushed the wind.

Verse 4:
In-to the cit-y I’d fol-low
The chil-dren’s band,
Wav-ing a branch of the palm tree
High in my hand.
One of His her-alds,
Yes, I would sing
Loud-est ho-san-nas,
Je-sus is King!

Verse 5:
Tell how the spar-row that twit-ters
On yon-der tree,
And the sweet mead-ow-side lil-y
May speak to me,
Give me their mes-sage,
For I would hear
How Je-sus taught us
Our Fa-ther’s care.

Verse 6:
Show me that scene in the gar-den,
Of bit-ter pain.
Show me the cross where my Sav-ior
For me was slain.
Sad ones or bright ones,
So that they be
Sto-ries of Je-sus,
Tell them to me.


---
Author: William H. Parker, 1885

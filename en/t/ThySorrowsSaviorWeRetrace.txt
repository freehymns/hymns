Thy Sorrows, Savior, We Retrace

Verse 1:
Thy sor-rows, Sav-ior, we re-trace,
And tears of praise Thy griefs com-pel.
What love and grace il-lume Thy face
As Je-sus, as Im-man-u-el!

Verse 2:
A-mid Thy lone-li-ness be-low,
What scorn and out-rage Thee be-fell:
Deep shame and woe, rude blow on blow,
En-dured for us, Im-man-u-el!

Verse 3:
But oh, what grief, what ag-o-ny,
When wrath-ful judg-ment’s aw-ful spell
Burst o-ver Thee on Cal-v’ry’s tree,
God’s Lamb for us, Im-man-u-el!

Verse 4:
A-ris-en rad-iant from the dead,
Thy sor-row’s scars for-ev-er tell,
Cre-a-tion’s Head is He who bled,
Still Je-sus, still Im-man-u-el!

Verse 5:
E’en now from saints, in con-cord sweet,
Ce-les-tial strains of wor-ship well;
For, O, ’tis meet glad songs should greet
Thy heart of love, Im-man-u-el!

Verse 6:
But when Thy glor-ious face we see,
How shall the burst-ing cho-rus swell!
Our souls shall be out-poured for Thee
Out-poured for Thee, Im-man-u-el!


---
Author: Frank Allaben (1867-1927)
Tune: Wareham

That Bright And Blessed Morn

Verse 1:
That bright and bless-èd morn is near
When He, the Bride-groom, shall ap-pear,
And call His bride a-way.
Her bless-ing then shall be com-plete,
When with her Lord she takes her seat
In ev-er-last-ing day.

Verse 2:
The days and months are glid-ing past,
Soon shall be heard the trump-et's blast
Which wakes the sleep-ing saints.
The dead in Christ in glo-ry rise,
When we with them shall reach the skies,
Where Je-sus for us waits.

Verse 3:
What won-der, joy, and glad sur-prise
Shall fill our hearts as thus we rise
To meet Him in the air;
To see His face, to hear His voice,
And in His per-fect love re-joice,
Whose glo-ry then we'll share!

Verse 4:
No more de-ferred our hope shall be,
No long-er through a glass we'll see,
But clear-ly, face to face.
We'll dwell with Je-sus then a-bove,
Whom ab-sent we have learn-ed to love,
Blest ob-jects of His grace.

Verse 5:
O may this hope our spir-its cheer,
While wait-ing for our Sav-ior here!
He'll quick-ly come a-gain.
O may our hearts look for that day,
And to His word re-spon-sive say,
"Come, Je-sus Lord, A-men."


---
Author: G. W. Frazer (1830-1896)
Tune: Meribah

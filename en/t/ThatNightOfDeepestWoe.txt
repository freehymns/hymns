That Night Of Deepest Woe

Verse 1:
'Twas on that night of deep-est woe,
When dark-ness round did thick-en,
When through deep wa-ters Thou didst go,
And for our sins wast strick-en;
Thou, Lord, didst seek that we should be
With grate-ful hearts re-mem-b'ring Thee.
Thou, Lord, didst seek that we should be
With grate-ful hearts re-mem-b'ring Thee.

Verse 2:
How deep the sor-row, who can tell,
Which was for us en-dur-èd,
O love di-vine, which broke the spell
Which had our hearts al-lur-èd.
With hearts and con-science now set free,
It is our joy to think of Thee.
With hearts and con-science now set free,
It is our joy to think of Thee.

Verse 3:
O Lord, how pre-cious is Thy thought,
How won-drous Thy de-si-re,
To win our hearts, once worse than naught,
Who now by grace as-pi-re
To seek Thy glo-ry, bear Thy shame,
To keep Thy word, and love Thy name!
To seek Thy glo-ry, bear Thy shame,
To keep Thy word, and love Thy name!

Verse 4:
We know Thee now, ex-alt-ed high,
Our-selves in Thee ac-cept-ed;
We wait the hour which now draws nigh,
Thy com-ing long ex-pect-ed.
Till Thou dost come, we still would be
With grate-ful hearts re-mem-b'ring Thee!
Till Thou dost come, we still would be
With grate-ful hearts re-mem-b'ring Thee!


---
Author: James G. Deck (1807-1884)
Tune: Maryland

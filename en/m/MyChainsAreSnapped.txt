My Chains Are Snapped

Verse 1:
My chains are snapped, the bonds of sin are bro-ken,
And I am free;
O let the tri-umphs of His grace be spo-ken,
Who died for me!

Verse 2:
O death! O grave! I do not dread your pow-er,
The ran-som’s paid;
On Je-sus, in that dark and dread-ful hour,
My guilt was laid.

Verse 3:
Yes, Je-sus bore it, bore, in love un-bound-ed,
What none can know;
He passed through death, and glo-rious-ly con-found-ed
Our ev-’ry foe.

Verse 4:
And now He’s ris’n, pro-claim the joy-ful sto-ry,
The Lord’s on high;
And we, in Him, are raised to end-less glo-ry,
And ne’er can die!

Verse 5:
We wait to see the Morn-ing Star ap-pear-ing
In glo-ry bright;
This bless-èd hope il-lumes, with beams most cheer-ing,
The hours of night!

---
Author: Margaret L. Carson
Tune: Palm
